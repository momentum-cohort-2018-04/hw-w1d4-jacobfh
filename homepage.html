About Me

A Short Story

My name is Jacob Heivly. I am a 24 year old, aspiring software developer from Chapel Hill, North Carolina. I grew up as a hyperactive child constantly wanting to try something new. Pretty soon I found a passion for skateboarding. I was able to enjoy this activity by my rules. Skateboarding taught me to never give up. We beat ourselves up for hours to achieve goals. Try, adjust, try again... (sounds a lot like coding, right?). After a decade of skateboarding I packed up my belongings to move west into the mecca of the American skate scene, Southern California. Living solo in California was a reality check to say the least. It's an incredibly competitive economy and the cost of living is out of this world compared to North Carolina. I learned to cut costs by any means which involved: minimizing possessions, living in a van, living on a boat, and taking any work opportunity. Four years had past and I had found myself a decent job managing at a skate shop in historical Carlsbad, CA. Life was better than it had been, but I was still working paycheck to paycheck and hardly skateboarding, the passion that had brought me to San Diego. It was at this point that I knew I needed to make a major change.

Why did I choose Software Development?

The technical world isn't going away any time soon. All modern companies can be considered "tech companies", and without developers there is no "tech". These skills are in high demand. My decade-long involvement with the skateboard community led me to believe that software development would be a great fit for me. What does skateboarding have to do with coding? Skateboarders are relentless. We will give every ounce of energy, fall over and over to accomplish a goal that we set for ourselves. It's trial and error, just like development.

Towards the end of my recent stay in San Diego, I found Momentum. A fully immersive, three month program led by industry professionals; and it was ten minutes away from where I grew up! I plan to acquire the skills to understand both front and back end development in preparation for jobs in this industy. Excitement would be an understatement for how I feel about learning, building, and growing within the Durham technical community.
